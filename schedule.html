About

The "Evolutionary Algorithms and Machine Learning: Theory and Applications" summer school offers a comprehensive and intensive exploration of the intersection between evolutionary algorithms (EAs) and machine learning (ML). This program is designed to provide participants with both theoretical foundations and practical insights into how evolutionary strategies can enhance machine learning models and vice versa. Participants will delve into the principles of evolutionary algorithms, including genetic algorithms, genetic programming, and other optimization techniques that draw inspiration from natural evolution. The program covers how these algorithms can be used to solve complex optimization problems, optimize neural networks, or even evolve new machine learning models. Furthermore, the summer school incorporates hands-on sessions where attendees will apply these concepts in real- world scenarios, such as optimizing hyper parameters in ML models, designing adaptive learning systems, or tackling large-scale computational problems. Leading experts in the field will share their research and experiences, offering valuable perspectives on both the challenges and breakthroughs in this rapidly evolving dom ain. The summer school aims to foster a collaborative learning environment, where participants can engage in discussions, work on projects, and gain insights into the latest trends and future directions of research at the intersection of evolutionary algorithms and machine learning.
